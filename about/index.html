About

Posted at — Oct 2, 2022

Dulguun Gantumur

An IR student at George Mason University interested in topics ranging from linguistics, philosophy, and Central Asia.

Languages

I currently speak English, Mongolian, Mandarin Chinese fluently, and actively learning Azerbaijani with plans of Russian or Uyghur in the future. In the past I have studied Esperanto, Persian, and Hungarian.

I participated in the Critical Languages Scholarship program in Taiwan to study Mandarin but largely undertook self-study beforehand, as I am currently doing so with Azerbaijani.

Philosophy

My current philosophical interests are in nationalism, ideology, post-modernism, and communist theory. Contemporary mainstream views, especially those grounded in modernity are accepted dogmatically by most members of society, and it is important to look critically what inadvertent effects modern systems may have. This has led to me to explore what the non-mainstream views are about society concerning modernity are, ranging from communist theory to other realms.

Central Asia

I regard the country of my birth, Mongolia, to be Central Asian, and seek to understand Mongolia’s significance to Central Asia and vice versa. Inquiry into these topics bright my previous interests of linguistics and philosophy into synthesis which I share through my short posts here and my longer research.
